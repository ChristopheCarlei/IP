## Initial situation

During this seminar, the important thing is that students can test their theoretical knowledge by experiencing a negotiation situation. The teachers have therefore decided to offer students the opportunity to experience a negotiation simulation with all the constraints that current events can bring to it. This format allows students to understand a crisis or an international political problem and to test their reactivity and creativity during the negotiation simulation.

## Project implementation

Students are required to work in teams, each representing a particular actor at the negotiating table. The negotiation exercise aims to be as realistic as possible by creating delegations and drafting negotiation mandates presenting the official position of the states/organizations around the tables. The theme of the negotiation simulation changes each year according to current political events, allowing students to be confronted with a contemporary issue.
During this seminar, 2 face-to-face sessions are organized. The first one, after a theoretical presentation of the negotiation method, allows students to see their team being formed by drawing lots. The actual negotiation takes place during the second session. After the teams (called delegations in the simulation) have been formed, the students organize themselves autonomously by assigning the different roles that make up a delegation (chairperson, assistant, observer, analyst, etc.). They are then asked to write a negotiation mandate in which they detail their negotiation strategy, which must be as close as possible to the official position of the country they will represent. During their preparation, students have the opportunity to interact with real actors, who come to present their position during the preparatory sessions, and thus benefit from direct access to sources.
During the simulation, the negotiation tables are chaired by experienced diplomats who ensure that the negotiations run smoothly. Students must be creative in finding common, realistic and acceptable solutions for all parties. They are therefore encouraged to actively participate in the exchanges and discussions, allowing them to really use and apply their knowledge.

## Thoughts and advice

Since most students only see each other virtually, it has become important over the years to draw negotiation teams as early as possible in the semester. This is to allow time for delegations to create a group identity using the communication medium of their choice (WhatsApp in the case of this course). This minimizes the effects of free riding and allows for a more balanced and, above all, more efficient division of workload.
