## Initial situation

The ever-increasing number of students, especially in the first year of a Bachelor's degree, is overloading the auditoriums. This sometimes compromises the learning and teaching conditions. In order to remedy this situation, the possibility of following courses remotely and live (streaming) has been offered to first year Bachelor students.

## Project implementation

In concrete terms, during the classroom sessions, the teacher is filmed. In the video, he appears in an insert in his PowerPoint or other presentation. Thus, the fact of seeing the teacher present the course and following his gestures brings an additional dynamic dimension compared to the Mediaserver recordings which only present the slides. This is greatly appreciated by the students. Despite the distance, the course is livelier.
At the same time, it is possible for the class to ask questions online directly to the teacher through complementary streaming tools such as SpeakUp. In order to encourage teacher-student interactions by stimulating proactivity, it is also possible to implement online voting methods such as Pingo, in which students can participate either in person or remotely.
Thoughts and advice on implementing a similar project
The impact on the number of students in the audience is quite small (10 to 15% of students use streaming). Nevertheless, it is a great tool when students are unable to attend class due to illness or other reasons.
A second important benefit of this innovation is that students with attentional difficulties or dyslexia use the streaming videos directly in the audience. This allows them to increase their concentration by focusing on the course being taught, while at the same time allowing them to benefit from the effect of the presence of their peers. This presence helps them focus on key moments in the course (e.g., when they see their colleagues' intensive note-taking).
It is essential for anyone wishing to implement such an innovation in their teaching to inform students about its potential effects on student performance. In fact, a study conducted in 2016-2017 showed that the use of streaming accentuated the gap in exam performance between students who had done well before coming to university and those who had done poorly. While high achieving students do better on the exam by using streaming, low achieving students do worse. It is therefore essential to inform students about these risks. It is necessary to make students aware of their responsibilities: if they have difficulties with autonomy, it is not advised to use this course format.

## Student feedback

> "Better comfort: With large numbers of students and crowded auditoriums, taking the class in a more comfortable location helps you learn better. Choosing when to watch the lecture allows you to choose the time when you feel most comfortable to follow correctly and understand better. Streaming teaching gives the possibility to listen to the teacher's explanations again, which is of incomparable help. It allows you to catch up on a class if you are unable to attend (sick, appointment, etc.).
> "I am able to understand and assimilate a much greater amount of information than when I go to class. My concentration is much better during a streaming course."
> "There is no need to travel to take the class, useful when you are sick."
> "Great idea whether it's for students who can't come or those who want to review the course. Personally, I go to all the classes, but I have also taken them all in streaming which has allowed me to understand the majority of the classes better."
