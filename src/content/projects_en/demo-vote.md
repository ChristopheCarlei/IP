## Initial situation

The course in the large lecture hall lacked rhythm. The great passivity of the students prevented effective learning.

Project implementation

Use by the teacher of Openboard, a software allowing to write live, to annotate the presentation. This will allow to pace the learning and to be more interactive with the students.

Online voting is also implemented with the Pingo platform. A way to make students work in groups, displaying answers in live.

Both tools will encourage student participation, they will quickly find out during the course what they have understood or not (and not once they have revised their course at home).

Thoughts and advice on implementing a similar project

These 2 technologies are very easy to use, the teacher adopted them because they met a need with a large workforce. They can be adopted without having to dramatically change the course. If you like the technology, then you can gradually rethink the way you structure and teach your course to move towards more interaction.

One disadvantage of Openboard is that it has made me more static in my teaching. Be careful not to abuse the use of Pingo so as not to chop up your lesson too much, you need to find the right rhythm!

## Project implementation

The inverted classroom format invites students to watch the videos of the MOOC "Borders of all kinds" before each seminar session (2 hours) and to complete the activities and assessments included in the MOOC. The sessions are designed to deepen the subject matter by using a wide variety of visual documents (photographs, paintings, maps, art installations, films, etc.) and take place as often as possible in the field.  In addition, external speakers from the academic, institutional or associative worlds are invited to participate in the various sessions, particularly when field visits are involved. The speakers guide the visit and the students actively participate in the investigation of the subject matter, particularly in terms of urban boundaries, through open discussions. They are invited to observe and talk with the actors in the field. For example, the visit to the Jewish cemetery in Vernier took place with the janitor. Another example is a visit to a nightclub with the manager of the establishment.

In the fall of 2018, the exhibition "Frontiers of all kinds" organized by the Department of Geography of the University of Geneva took place. The exhibition was an integral part of the innovative device set up in the framework of the seminar. In addition to participating in the MOOC and the field visits, the students had the opportunity to produce a panel as a group to complement the exhibition. Only panels that were sufficiently completed were actually added to the exhibition.

When there is no exhibition linked to the seminar, the evaluation of learning is conditioned by the complete completion of the MOOC, participation in the seminar sessions and possibly a final essay on a substantive question related to the course theme. The exhibition is not renewable each year for budgetary reasons. However, developments for future exhibitions and collaborations on other projects are planned. For example, the teacher also participates in the poliScope, which bridges the gap between the city (more particularly the students) and the UNIGE, and one of the projects was "Frontier of gender: at school". In this context, it is considering an evolution on thematic hooks and with multimedia components for the students' productions.

Each year a new theme is introduced by the teaching team (e.g. water security). A theoretical introduction is provided by the teacher but also by speakers from the professional, political and UN world.

The students are then divided into groups (4-5 members) in order to maximize interdisciplinarity and plurality of skills. Each group identifies a problem related to the proposed theme for which they will develop an innovative solution (e.g. how to raise awareness among Swiss youth about the water footprint of their fashionable clothes). The solutions proposed by the groups can be technical (e.g. application prototype) or propositional (e.g. public policy recommendation) and also linked to the sustainable development objectives.

Each week, workshops offer students the opportunity to discover and integrate new tools and concepts that they will need (e.g. systemic analysis, change theory, project management, presentation...). From the analysis of the problem to the final report describing the solution, the groups work independently and are supervised throughout the process by a mentor from the teaching team. The mentor ensures the coherence of the proposals, accompanies the reflection at different stages and tries to find the balance between innovation/creativity and operationalization of the project.

Students are evaluated on their active participation in the workshops (10%), the 3 sessions of oral presentations of the group projects (20%) and on their written project proposal (70%). An opportunity is offered to particularly innovative groups to further develop their projects by obtaining seed funding from the startup incubator funded by the Geneva Tsinghua Initiative.

## Thoughts and advice

The implementation of such a pedagogical project necessarily implies the availability of a working field for the students. The teacher also recommends that field trips be well prepared in advance with the systematic help of a facilitator. This facilitates guidance during the visit and encourages the analysis of a non-academic discourse. This makes it possible to take full advantage of the speaker's expertise and, for the teacher, to maintain the pedagogical role during the session.

A written restitution of each session or an additional production for an exhibition constitutes in both cases too heavy a workload for a 3 ETCS credit seminar, as participation in the MOOC is an initial condition for the validation of the seminar. Furthermore, the teacher recommends in all cases the programming of one or two sessions on a specific subject interspersed over the total duration of the MOOC in order to verify the students' follow-up and their progress in the course and the theoretical notions. In general, the follow-up of the students can also be done in person at each seminar session, but it is important to verify that the notions presented in the MOOC are also integrated.

Concerning the student productions for the exhibition, it is important to keep in mind that the workload is important. The teacher recommends the creation of work groups to dilute the workload in part, but also to boost competition and encourage creativity. However, it is important not to give false hope as to the display of the productions, since only the most successful ones are included in the current exhibition, and to remember that the display or not of the production is not part of the evaluation. When it is inserted in an exhibition, the type of production is imposed, but the rest of the time, the choice of the output to produce could be variable to leave more amplitude to the students.

The implementation of such a project requires a lot of organization upstream and once launched, there is little room for maneuver. Each year, a new theme must be found, external contributors must be found and a lot of time must be set aside for coordination and student follow-up.

It is important to give very clear instructions to the students about the program and the expected deliverables. This strong structuring of the work process is not incompatible with creativity and the search for innovative solutions.

The course, its implementation, the management of the agenda in case of constraints (health) is really time-consuming and deserves a more important support.

Would like to have a call for projects platform that could be broken down by topic and faculty.

The implementation of the project: the teacher really wanted to make sure that everything was going well, and took on a large part of the follow-up on project management. For future batches, the teacher now wants to rethink to lighten the teacher's load and think of new tools to facilitate project follow-up.
