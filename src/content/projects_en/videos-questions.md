## Initial situation

The physics course for first year medical students has 2 major constraints: on the one hand, the number of students is large with more than 600, and on the other hand, there is a lot of material to teach. In these conditions, it is difficult to know during the course what the students really understand and therefore to adjust the pace. It is also difficult to get the students out of their passive posture in the audience.

## Project implementation

This project has two objectives.

The first one is to make sure that all students have a basic knowledge of mathematics before taking the course. To do this, the teacher himself developed short video clips using a document camera on basic mathematical tools essential in physics, which students are supposed to know at the beginning of their first year of their bachelor's degree in medicine. Students can watch these videos at their own pace and ensure that they are able to follow the course.

The second is to keep the students engaged during the lecture. At several points during the course, the teacher stops the lecture and asks the students a question (multiple choice). The question is answered using their smartphone. The teacher sees the results live and can then decide whether to spend more time on the concept in question or whether to move on to the next one. In addition to asking students questions, the teacher also uses the SpeakUp plateform developed by UNIL which allows students to ask questions live and to vote for the most relevant questions. The teacher can thus adapt his course according to the questions asked via this platform. To change the pace of the course and to get more attention, the teacher also shows video clips of experiments conducted in physics during the course.

## Thoughts and advice

The video vignettes offered in the flipped classroom allow the teacher to directly address the course material without wasting time reviewing basic concepts that were supposedly learned in high school.

In addition to allowing the teacher to assess the level of understanding of the whole class, in-class voting encourages students to take a more active role in the course. Indeed, students informally exchange with each other on the questions during the reflection time. The answers given are therefore often those of small groups (although this is not explicitly stated). Online voting also allows students to better assess their understanding of what remains to be studied.

Student feedback

"The interactivity of the course is quite nice thanks to SpeakUp. The teacher also has a way of capturing the attention of the students, which I think is essential in learning, especially in this course. The presence of exercises that we have to participate in during the class through online voting is, in my opinion, very helpful in the assimilation of new concepts."

"The frequent questions asked during the course by the teacher, which we can answer interactively are a strong point. Moreover, the possibility to ask our own questions via SpeakUp is really a chance: we can ask the questions directly, and the whole audience can benefit from the explanations."

"The video examples regarding the physics chapter, it's a good idea, they help us understand some chapters better."
